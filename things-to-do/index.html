Things to See

  • Look at Georgian Houses near Fitzwilliam Square and Merrion Square.
  • Kilmainham Gaol Tour (near Guinness if you are going there).
  • Instead of the bus tour, take the Viking Splash tour.
  • Ireland has a history in literature. Visit the National Library (for WB Yeats) and the Séamus Heaney museum (look out for Róisín's uncle).
  • For Dublin's modern architecture, visit the Docklands and Grand Canal Dock.
  • If you want a taste of Irish humour, visit the International Bar for comedy.
  • Dublin Castle and grounds.
  • You will see Trinity College on the wedding day!
You might consider getting the 3-day Dublin Bikes bike-sharing subscription for five euro to get around easier.

Places to Eat

Breakfast

  • Hatch & Sons on Stephen's Green. Here you can get Blaas from Waterford, Christoph's home county, which are very soft and floury bread rolls. They are one of the few geographically-protected food products from Ireland.
  • Try a breakfast roll from any shop, a full Irish breakfast in a baguette.
  • If you have self-catering, buy white pudding from FX Buckley Butcher.

Coffee

Third floor espresso, Coffee Angel, Kaph, Clement and Pekoe, Avoca Cafe (try their scones!)
Pepper Pot for tea (they give you the option of Lyons or Barry's)

Lunch

Hatch & Sons on Stephen's Green (see under Breakfast)
Avoca Cafe
A chicken fillet roll from a Spar or Centra (Westland Row Centra is a good option).

Dinner

For Irish food, O'Niell's on Suffolk Street is supposed to be good.
For seafood, take the DART to Howth and do the cliff-walk, then eat fish.
For steak, FX Buckley is supposed to be good.
Try Dublin Bay Prawns, they are like baby lobsters.

Late Night Supper

Fish and chips (Beschoff's is the best, Leo Burdock's is also good).
ZAYTOON for Persian cuisine.
Replenish carbohydrates with a 3-in-1 from any Chinese Take-Away (substituting curry sauce with sweet and sour is often possible).
You can also get late-night chicken fillet rolls in the George's Street Spar.

Places to Drink

For pints of stout:
  • Grogan's Licensed Premiess. Flann O'Brien speaks fondly of the pub
  • Toner's Bar (get in the snug on the left as you enter, daytime drinking is essential to get a seat in it)
  • Kehoe's Bar (get in the snug on the left as you enter)
  • Bowes Bar
  • O'Donoghues Bar, Merrion Row
For Irish traditional music (check for times in advance):
  • McNeill's Pub on Capel Street
  • Walsh's in Stoneybatter
  • Cobblestone in Stoneybatter
For Irish craft beer:
  • Black Sheep
  • Against the Grain
  • The Gingerman
  • If you visit the Guinness Brewery, the Guinness Open Gate Brewery has experimental pints.
For late night drinkers (after 1am):
  • Whelan's
  • Workman's Club
  • Copper Face Jacks
